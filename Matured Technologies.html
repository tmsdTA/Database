Rice Husk Gas Stove

Rice husk gas stove employs the principle of gasification. Gasification is a process of converting solid fuels, such as rice husk into a useful gas with restricted supply of air. In the rice husk gasifier stove, the rice husk is heated under limited amount of air or in a state of partial combustion that result in the production of gases such as carbon monoxide (CO), hydrogen (H2 ), and methane (CH4 ). These are termed as producer gas, and is combustible. It produces bluish flame when burnt due to the low carbon content of the gas. Char is a by-product of the gasification process.

Maligaya Flatbed Dryer

The Maligaya Flatbed dryer is a batch-type mechanical dryer that is used to remove the moisture content of the grains down to a safe level for storing and further processing. It uses rice husk as heat source. The Maligaya Flatbed Dryer is used to dry crops such as corn, coffee, legumes, rice, and other plants or products that need to be dried (like banana and cassava chips). It uses rice husks, corn stalks, or coffee husks as fuel to heat the air inside the dryer through a blower. The dryer can be used even during rainy weather or at night.

Saclob

Saclob comes from the Filipino word saklob, which means “cover”. The saclob is a locally-developed airtight container, equipped with a simple sealing mechanism, where bags of rice seeds are placed during storage. Its development started in 2004 owing to an urgent need of the government's Hybrid Rice Commercialization Program for a low cost alternative to an effective but imported hermetic technology used in storing hybrid rice seeds. Results of a three-year study that evaluated the performance of saclob showed that it can preserve the quality of stored seeds much better than the conventional open storage and comparably with its imported counterpart.

Bamboo-bin dryer

The Bamboo-bin Dryer consists of three main parts: 1. the drying bin made of two concentric bamboo mats (the bigger one stores grains; the smaller one, wrapped around a steel bar frame which serves as plenum); 2. the blower, with small electric motor and automotive fan blade which provides the drying air; and 3. the heater, 2-kW resistor coil, which slightly raises the temperature of drying air.

Maligaya Rice Hull Stove

The concentric design and the presence of air holes make the Maligaya Rice Hull Stove efficient. The holes of the hopper cone at the lower portion allow air into the flame cone during combustion while the holes on the flame cone allow the flame to be diverted toward the center. The smoke cone directs the smoke, which is also flammable, toward the center. This makes the heat more concentrated and the stove almost smokeless
