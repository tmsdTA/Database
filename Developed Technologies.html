Capillary Irrigation System

This technology is described by its generators as a system of irrigating rice-based crops that is almost similar with the drip irrigation system, but one which makes use of capillary wicks instead of drippers. Conceptualized for the use of small-holder rice-based farmers, the Capillarigation system utilizes local materials and is said to be simple enough to be established by the farmers themselves.

Multi-Purpose Riding-type Mini-tractor (Makisig)

This machine is designed to perform functions that are beyond the capability of existing hand tractors and 4W mini-tractors so as to help farmers cope up with the various challenges caused by climate change. In particular, it is designed to be able to work in deep mud-conditions, which happens to rice fields affected by floods and landslides. In addition to land preparation, it is also designed to perform functions like digging canals for conveying water, for constructing small ponds for rainwater harvesting, and drilling shallow tubewells.

Combined Conduction and Far Infrared Radiation Paddy Dryer

A new rapid drying technology for paddy using a combined conduction and far infrared radiant heating process. The combined principles of conduction and far-infrared radiation can be a major breakthrough in the field of paddy drying. The use of rice husk gasification technology for providing heat energy requirements would entirely eliminate the high cost of and dependence on imported fossil fuel.

Aquaculture feed ingredient from blue -green algae (BGA)

This aqualcuture feed ingredient is made from fibrous algae present in inland water resources such as ponds and rice paddies in combination with commercial fish-feeds. Nile tilapia fed with fibrous algae has 38.85% growth percentage and 91.67% survival at 25:75 algae and commercial feed ratio. This technology hopes to improve the economic status of farmers by mass producing the algae that can be collected from ponds and rice paddies and process them into an aquaculture feed ingredient.

Rice Bran Noodles

Noodles are convenient wheat-based food popularly consumed throughout the world rich in carbohydrate but usually deficient in essential nutrients which are lost during wheat refinement. Rice bran, an agricultural by-product abundant in fiber, antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, was utilized as a functional ingredient for noodles to maximize its use for the prevention of lifestyle diseases linked to poor intake of dietary fiber and carbohydrates. The process includes (1) rice bran preparation - dehulling and polishing of rice; and (2) Noodles preparation – mixing the dough, kneading and flatting, cutting, and cooking. Supplementation of fresh wheat noodles with 10% rice bran significantly improved the dietary fiber, phenolic content, and total antioxidant capacity of the product without affecting the overall sensory acceptability.

Bamboo-bin Dryer

The Bamboo-bin Dryer consists of three main parts: 1. the drying bin made of two concentric bamboo mats (the bigger one stores grains; the smaller one, wrapped around a steel bar frame which serves as plenum); 2. the blower, with small electric motor and automotive fan blade which provides the drying air; and 3. the heater, 2-kW resistor coil, which slightly raises the temperature of drying air.

Complementary Food

To help improve nutrition in these areas of weaning period as a crucial time of an infant’s life, this study developed a plant-based complementary food premix from rice, soybean, and sweet potato for older infants aged 6-23 months. Ten complementary food premixes were formulated and optimized using D-optimal mixture design. Results showed that different proportion of flour blends had significant (p < 0.05) effects on protein, ash, fat, carbohydrates, water absorption index, swelling power, and consistency. The complementary food formulation with optimized nutrient quality, hydration properties, and consumer acceptance was obtained.

Rice ICT-Based Sensory System

The Rice ICT-Based Sensory System (RISS) was initially developed in 2022 in partnership with the IT experts from the Information Systems Division to automate the tedious paper-based sensory evaluation of sensory attributes of milled rice and cooked grains. In 2023, some key features of the system were improved, and actual performance was validated. At present, the RISS is now fully operational, accessible via the intranet, and effectively supports the routine evaluation of sensory characteristics of rice and rice-based food products.

Rice Grain Dimensions Measuring App

The dimensions of rice grains play a crucial role in determining consumer preferences and commercial value. Grain length and shape also influence rice grain yield and milling recovery. Improving these traits is one of the targets of the rice breeding programs. The caliper-based method can be labor-intensive and often subjective, and prone to human error. To address these issues, we have developed a smartphone-based application (App) that employs computer vision and deep learning to evaluate rice grain dimensions. A 3D-printed platform securely positions the smartphone at a calibrated distance above a grain holder to ensure accurate measurements. The App captures images of multiple grains simultaneously, which are then analyzed to determine the length (L), width (W), and shape (L/W) of each grain based on pixel dimensions and a pre-calibrated pixel-to-distance ratio. The initial validation test of 146 rice samples showed a prediction accuracy of 98%, with analysis time reduced from 10 minutes to under two minutes compared to the conventional method. Pilot testing has already been performed in other research laboratories to assess the app’s reliability and accuracy further. The App demonstrated good performance by achieving a 96% accuracy in predicting the grain length and shape of 22 rice samples evaluated by the CLSU-PhilSCAT laboratory analysts. In a separate evaluation conducted at PhilMech, the App’s prediction accuracy was 97% when tested with 31 unknown rice samples.

Grain Quality- Rice Information System

The Grain Quality – Rice Information System (GQ-RIS) serves as a central repository for information on rice grain quality, which includes details about milling recovery, physical attributes, physicochemical properties, and sensory characteristics. The system aims to provide an internet-accessible platform for efficient storage, retrieval, visualization, and management of rice grain quality data. Currently, the GQ-RIS contains relevant grain quality information for all Philippine-approved rice varieties, as well as NCT and pre-NCT rice lines. The GQ-RIS is in the testing phase, following an agile methodology. The user interface is under review while the Development Communication Team is proofreading the content. We are also updating the database and enhancing additional features to better meet user needs and expectations.
